Rationale




To be more specific about the questions I wish to answer:

  1. Is true mass surveillance and data sifting reasonably possible merely in terms of technology?
  2. Can there be both actionable mass surveillance (acted on by an entity in the name of safety) and liberty?
  3. Is our incarceration system inadequate, and how should it change if so?
  4. How would a justice system change with mass surveillance?
  5. Is an aristocracy actually a feasible form of government, and what would make someone fit to rule?
  6. Can corruption and misuse of power be prevented in those individual systems?

The ways in which each of these questions relate to that which was brought up in the introduction should be clear;
if mass surveillance is not reasonably possible, then it could not create a better society. If it conflicts with
liberty, then it is inadequate. If there is a better system of incarceration, then what we have needs to change;
especially if mass surveillance changed the severity of punishment. If a system of rule could be better than what
presently exists in terms of the ability to act, react, and remain stable, then it would be very beneficial to the
people to switch to such a system. Finally, if corruption is not almost certainly preventable, then the government
